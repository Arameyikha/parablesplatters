how to fly a commercial airplane

pre-flight checks

  • inspect the aircraft exterior: check for any damage, debris, or anomalies. Ensure the wings, tail, and fuselage are intact.
  • check control surfaces: verify that ailerons, elevators, and rudder move freely.
  • check tires and landing gear: ensure tires are inflated and the landing gear is in good condition.
  • fuel check: verify fuel levels and inspect for any leaks.
  • pitot tube: make sure it's clear of obstructions.
  • aircraft lights: check that all lights (navigation, strobe, landing) are operational.
  • seats and seat belts: ensure all are properly adjusted and secure.
  • emergency equipment: check that fire extinguishers, first aid kits, and other emergency gear are in place.
  • instruments: verify that all necessary instruments are working correctly, including the altimeter, compass, and engine gauges.
  • circuit breakers: ensure none are popped.
  • radio and communications: test the radios and ensure proper functionality.
  • autopilot system: check for proper operation if using it during the flight.
  • air conditioning/heating: verify if needed.
  • turn on avionics: power up the avionics systems and check for proper operation.
  • systems check: confirm that all systems (electric, hydraulic, pneumatic, etc.) are functioning properly.

engine start-up

  • turn the ignition switch to start the engine. Allow engines to warm up and reach optimal operating temperatures.
  • close doors: ensure that all doors are securely closed before taxiing.
  • set throttle: apply gentle throttle to begin taxiing.

takeoff

  • set throttle: apply full throttle for takeoff.
  • monitor speed: the aircraft will accelerate, reaching takeoff speed (typically around 80-100 knots for small aircraft).
  • lift-off: as the aircraft reaches the appropriate speed, slowly pull back on the yoke (control column) to raise the nose and lift off from the runway.
  • retract landing gear: once airborne and clear of the runway, retract the landing gear.
  • climb to desired altitude: use the yoke to maintain control and set climb rate. Adjust throttle as necessary to climb smoothly.
  • set autopilot: once in cruise mode and stable at altitude, engage autopilot for hands-off flying.

in-flight procedures

  • monitor systems: continuously check engine parameters and flight instruments for any anomalies.
  • navigation: ensure the route is correctly aligned and monitor the navigation systems.
  • altitude adjustments: adjust as necessary for changes in flight plan or air traffic control instructions.

descent and landing

  • descent preparation: reduce engine power to begin descending. Adjust trim and yoke as needed to maintain balance and stability.
  • lower landing gear: deploy the landing gear as you approach the runway.
  • final approach: continue to monitor airspeed, altitude, and heading. Perform any necessary checks to ensure a safe approach.
  • touchdown: gently lower the aircraft onto the runway, ensuring a smooth landing.
  • reverse thrust: apply reverse thrust to slow down the aircraft as needed.
  • braking: use the rudder pedals for braking to bring the aircraft to a full stop.

post-flight procedures

  • shut down engines: turn off the engine and avionics.
  • systems off: ensure all systems (fuel, electrical, hydraulic) are turned off.
  • post-flight checks: inspect the aircraft once more for any issues or damage.
  • open doors and exit: open the aircraft doors and exit safely.
  • lock up: secure the aircraft by locking the doors and securing any necessary items.

final steps

  • post-flight logbook entry: record flight details, maintenance checks, and any issues encountered during the flight.
  • debriefing: if flying with a crew, debrief the flight to discuss performance, issues, and anything that needs attention for future flights.
